# Team Explanation Script — DFP 2.0 Scoring Method

## Opening

“We are not asking Program Managers to search the internet from scratch, and we are not asking them to judge an NGO from its homepage.

For every NGO, the research layer first identifies the relevant website pages and annual reports. The PM is then guided through the exact source evidence and receives a short factual package. Only after seeing that evidence does the PM give a score.”

## The three dimensions

“Every NGO is assessed on three separate dimensions.

**Alumni Outcomes** asks: What have former students gone on to achieve?

**Learning Model** asks: How are children actually taught, trained and prepared to progress?

**Development Environment** asks: What sustained support and opportunities surround the child beyond instruction?

The three dimensions are kept separate. The same claim cannot increase two scores.”

## What the Kalkeri tutorial demonstrates

“In the practice tutorial, we assess only Kalkeri’s Alumni Outcomes.

The PM is taken to Kalkeri’s real website and annual-report archive. The relevant report pages are highlighted. Four factual sentences are collected:

1. Sixty-five students were in higher education.
2. Ten students completed higher education that year, and Sangeeta completed a master’s and topped her batch.
3. Prakash completed an MBA and became a Service Delivery Manager at IndusInd Bank.
4. The report gives repeated transition rates from Class 10 to PUC, PUC to undergraduate study, and bachelor’s degrees to further study.

The PM then gives a rating without seeing the calibration.”

## Why Kalkeri is rated 4

“Kalkeri receives 4 because the evidence is strong and repeated. It includes aggregate numbers, movement across several educational stages and named alumni destinations.

It is clearly stronger than a 3, which would normally rely on a few examples or less systematic progression evidence.”

## Why Kalkeri cannot be rated 5

“The mandatory ceiling is the most important control.

A 5 requires broad, repeated and well-documented long-term destinations across multiple alumni cohorts.

Kalkeri has strong progression numbers and two excellent alumni profiles, but the public evidence does not give a complete multi-year picture of college completion and employment across most alumni. Therefore, even if the institution feels impressive overall, Alumni Outcomes is capped at 4.”

## How the method applies to the other dimensions

“Learning Model and Development Environment follow the same process, but they use different evidence rules and different mandatory ceilings.

For Learning Model, ordinary schooling, lessons and assessments cannot produce a high score. A high score requires embedded, intensive and clearly described instructional mechanisms.

For Development Environment, free education, uniforms and basic services do not differentiate an NGO. A high score requires integrated, sustained and unusually deep support around the child.

This prevents a long list of ordinary activities from becoming an inflated score.”

## Closing

“The final score is therefore not an impression. It is the end of a visible chain:

Source → factual evidence package → metric boundary → mandatory ceiling → PM rating → calibration and audit.

That complete chain is retained for every NGO, which makes the assessment consistent, reviewable and defendable.”
