Why Kalkeri receives 4

The evidence goes beyond isolated stories. It shows 65 students in higher education, 10 completions in one year, repeated progression from Class 10 into PUC and from PUC into undergraduate study, and named alumni reaching a master’s degree, teaching and a managerial role at IndusInd Bank.

Mandatory ceiling: why this cannot receive 5

A 5 requires broad, repeated and well-documented long-term destinations across multiple alumni cohorts. Kalkeri’s public material gives strong progression numbers and two excellent alumni profiles, but not a complete multi-year college-completion and employment picture across most former students. The score is therefore capped at 4.

Why not one point lower or higher?

Why not 3?↑

The evidence includes aggregate numbers, several transition stages and named destinations. It is materially stronger than a few isolated alumni anecdotes.

Why not 5?↓

The evidence does not yet provide broad longitudinal outcomes across several graduating cohorts or a comprehensive set of college and employment destinations.
